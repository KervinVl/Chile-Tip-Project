Easter Island

Easter Island is a Chilean island in the south Pacific Ocean, famous for the giant statues known
as Moais, which dot the landscape. Created by the Rapa Nui people, who are thought to have
inhabited the island from the 12th century onward, hundreds of Moais are spread around the
island. The natural beauty, stunning moai, and modern Polynesian culture are very much worth
the trip.

Pukara de Quitor

The Pre-Columbian archaeological site of Pukara de Quitor is a stone fortress in Chile's Atacama
Desert. Situated close to the desert town of San Pedro de Atacama, the ruins date to perhaps the
12th century AD. The walled city was ruled by the Inca until the 16th century, when the Spanish
struggled for two decades to subdue its inhabitants. The Pukara de Quitor is a worthwhile stop
while visiting the adorable hamlet of San Pedro de Atacama. This hillside fortress has an
interesting history, epic viewpoints, and some peculiar head sculptures.

Museum of Memory and Human Rights

The Museum of Memory and Human Rights is in Chile's capital city of Santiago and
commemorates the victims of human rights violations committed between 1973 and 1990.
During this time, Chile was ruled by the military dictator Augusto Pinochet. The museum was
inaugurated in 2010 by former President Michelle Bachelet. The museum houses testimony from
survivors of Pinochet's regime, letters to family members by prisoners in detention centers, as
well as torture devices.
